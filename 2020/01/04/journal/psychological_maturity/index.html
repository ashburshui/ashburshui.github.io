Psychological Maturity...

Jan 4, 2020

Just take the courage to confront all the difficulties in life.

Here is an image, which illustrates this very idea… Love it.

Remarkable year for me, the most important thing is that I regain confidence… More than that, I become more psychologically mature than before. Life is full of uncertainty, I can’t make precise prediction… But for me, personally speaking, really a significant year.

This article is very good for me… The name of it is 20 Signs You’re Emotionally Mature.

Tips
You realize people are never good or bad, they are traumatized and complex. Complexity is interesting.
You learn to articulate your emotions to get your point across.
You acknowledge that you are not perfect and learn to apologize.
You learn to be confident without being narcissistic.
You forgive your parents for fucking you up so, because you keep remembering they have gone through the same.
You make important decisions and initiate important conversations only when you are completely rested from various indirect stressors.
You forgive people who hurt you, even if they don’t understand after your explain why you were hurt.
You cease to believe in perfect life and perfect people.
You don’t keep your hopes up too high in general and learn to be comfortable with it.
You understand that other people’s weaknesses are always balanced by their strengths and learn to concentrate on them.
You don’t fall in love for superficial qualities.
You understand that you are just as big pain in the ass to be around with, as the other people seem to be.
You learn to forgive yourself regardless of how you suck at everything.
You make peace with an immature version of yours that shows itself once in a while.
You seek satisfaction in tiny achievements rather than in monumental ones.
You stop caring what others think of you.
You appreciate healthy criticism.
You make time to examine your life.
You are able to identify inner traumas you received as a child and avoid acting on impulses instilled by those experiences.
You understand that true friendship is about exchanging a personal story, rather than best advice.

Want to share with you guys… The main focus, I notice in this article, is that how can I become more responsible for both work and life.

Time again, I find that I should insist on the habit of reflection and gratitude. Because if you lose this trait, you will become arrogant very quickly… Remind myself, of being grateful for what happened…

From another perspective, if I can fully understand the essence of psychological maturity… I can behave according the following tips… 40 Signs You’ve Finally Learned To Love Yourself.

Tips
You’re not afraid to ask for what you want and you are not afraid to say what you don’t want.
You believe in yourself and you know that you are capable of achieving what you truly desire.
You hone your talents and your strengths and you don’t need external motivation to keep you going.
You stopped giving chances to people who keep letting you down.
You’ve moved on from toxic relationships and you are not looking back.
You started filtering your inner circle and detached yourself from the negative people and the ones who do not truly care about you.
You defend yourself and your ideas to those who belittle you.
You stand up for yourself when you need to instead of biting your tongue to please people.
You’re actively going to the gym and taking care of your body for yourself not for anyone else.
You’re crossing things off your bucket list and adding more incredible items.
You are spending more time doing things that you are passionate about.
You’re making new connections with people who inspire you.
You are closing old doors that lead to nowhere and you are finding new doors that lead to better places.
The voices in your head are a lot kinder and more supportive than they used to be.
You forgave yourself for your mistakes and tried to see the lessons in them.
You are more confident in yourself and in your abilities even when people are doubting you.
You know when to walk away from people who don’t appreciate you.
You are truly enjoying your life now and you are grateful for it.
You are more assertive in your decisions and in your feelings.
You are being real to the people around you and to yourself.
You are not afraid of saying sorry or apologizing.
You do not take rejection personally and you understand it is a part of life.
You put yourself first when you need to.
You don’t save things for special occasions or for certain people. You want to look and feel good all the time.
You are not afraid of shining or promoting yourself (in a healthy way.)
You genuinely feel happy for other people and you stopped comparing yourself to others.
You wake up every day ready to do something wonderful.
You stopped saying negative statements like ‘I’m a mess’ or ‘I’m not good enough.’
You turned your negative self-talk into positive affirmations.
You are calmer and you stopped letting the small things get to you.
You are becoming more spiritual and trusting the universe to bring you everything you want.
You respect yourself and you no longer tolerate people who don’t.
You know that your voice is important and it matters even if people don’t agree.
You are making plans and pursuing interests without waiting for someone to join you.
You’ve become the voice of optimism to your friends and family.
You want to learn more and are always looking for ways to be better a person.
You do not chase people or love or anything that obliges you to degrade yourself.
You smile more than you cry and you finally like your reflection in the mirror.
You no longer feel lonely because you actually like the person you’re alone with.
You believe that you deserve phenomenal love and you know you will find it.

When you really understand these invaluable principles… Then you can enjoy life much more…

时刻想着, 并不断问自己一个问题, “有限的精力, 有限的生命, 我应该坚持哪些事, 放弃哪些事, 才不枉世上走一遭?”
